## Working Assumptions

- Brand name: 原上禾 / YUANSHANGHE
- Category: Northwest grain, noodle, and regional pantry food brand
- Hero products: hand-pulled noodles, millet, wheat flour, chili oil, and seasonal gift packs
- Audience: urban families and young professionals buying trustworthy regional food online and in boutique grocery stores
- Price tier: refined everyday, with a premium gift line
- Personality: grounded, warm, clean, regional, professional, not rustic
- Key touchpoints: package front/back, SKU system, e-commerce hero images, social posts, shipping box, gift box, retail shelf strip

## Strategy

```text
design_direction: Northwest Field-to-Table Identity
brand_positioning: modern regional food brand that translates Northwest grain culture into clean daily pantry products
target_audience: quality-sensitive urban consumers who want real regional origin without old-fashioned local-specialty packaging
visual_metaphor: field line + grain kernel + food vessel + road from origin to table
industry_style_logic: food packaging must be appetizing, legible, trustworthy, and easy to extend across SKUs
geometry_system: modular field lines, circular bowl/plate forms, single grain dot, grid-based wordmark spacing
color_system: flour paper base, deep ink, millet gold, chili clay, mineral gray, small green freshness accent only when needed
typography_system: confident modern Chinese serif or hybrid Songti for brand name; simple grotesk for Latin, nutrition, and product data
imagery_style: natural ingredient photography with warm side light, matte paper, clean surfaces, no crowded rustic props
packaging_logic: large product name, seal/icon mark, SKU color band, back-panel information grid, clear origin story block
aesthetic_reference: premium East Asian food packaging, Swiss editorial grids, restrained regional material palette
avoid_list: tourist souvenir aesthetics, heavy ethnic patterns, distressed vintage paper, camel/mountain cliché, cartoon mascot, generic organic leaf
```

## Design Criteria

The best direction should:

- feel food-specific, not a generic cultural logo
- signal Northwest region through material and form, not clichés
- be simple enough to vectorize after image approval
- scale into packaging, social avatar, and shelf systems
- leave room for premium and everyday product lines
